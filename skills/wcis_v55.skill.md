## PILLAR 1 — GEOPOLITICAL CONFLICT

*Theater-level force analysis. Who has decision superiority and why.*

### Core Framework: Decision Superiority Model

**Boyd's OODA Loop Applied to Conflict:**
Decision superiority is not about having more forces. It is about cycling through Observe → Orient → Decide → Act faster than the adversary. The force that consistently completes the OODA loop faster wins — regardless of material advantage. Every conflict assessment must map which actor has the faster loop and why.

**Clausewitz: The Trinity of War:**
War is governed by three forces simultaneously — primordial violence/passion (the people), probability/chance (the military), and rational purpose (the government). An actor whose trinity is misaligned — e.g., government objectives diverge from military capacity or popular will — is structurally degraded regardless of firepower. Map the trinity for every actor before assessing capability.

**Mearsheimer: Offensive Realism:**
Great powers do not stop acquiring power until they achieve hegemony. Defensive postures are temporary. Every territorial or diplomatic concession must be assessed against the offensive realist baseline: is this actor consolidating or preparing? Stated intentions are not intelligence. Capability + posture is intelligence.

**WCIS Confidence Calibration for P1:**

| Confidence Level | Criteria |
|---|---|
| HIGH | Multiple independent open-source confirmations + doctrinal consistency |
| MODERATE | Single sourced + behavioral pattern consistent |
| LOW | Contested reporting + ideological framing risk |
| UNVERIFIED | Single source, no corroboration — flag explicitly |

## PILLAR 2 — CYBER WARFARE

*Attribution methodology, capability assessment, escalation modeling.*

### Attribution Framework

**The Attribution Problem:**
Cyber attribution is not binary. It exists on a spectrum: tactical attribution (IP/infrastructure), operational attribution (TTPs/tooling), strategic attribution (nation-state sponsorship). Each level requires different evidence and carries different confidence ceilings.

**Five Eyes Attribution Standard:**
Only accept attribution claims that meet: (1) consistent TTP fingerprint, (2) infrastructure overlap with known state actors, (3) targeting pattern consistent with stated adversary interests, (4) corroborated by at least two independent intelligence sources.

**Escalation Ladder:**
```
Tier 1 → Espionage / collection (lowest escalation)
Tier 2 → Pre-positioning / access operations
Tier 3 → Disruptive attacks (operational impact)
Tier 4 → Destructive attacks (physical consequence)
Tier 5 → Critical infrastructure targeting (near-kinetic threshold)
```

Every cyber incident must be placed on this ladder before escalation risk is assessed.

### P2-SDO — Space Domain Operations Sub-Node ★ v5.2

*Satellite ISR as kill chain enabler. Commercial satellite weaponization via civil-military fusion. Ground station relay architecture as adversary ISR backbone.*

**Satellite ISR as Kill Chain Enabler:**
Modern precision strike is satellite-dependent. The kill chain — from target detection to weapons employment — runs through commercial and military ISR satellites. Adversary targeting of satellite infrastructure is not an abstract threat; it is a direct attack on the sensor-to-shooter pipeline. Every theater conflict assessment must map which satellite constellations are providing ISR support and whether those assets are contested.

**Commercial Satellite Weaponization via Civil-Military Fusion:**
The PRC's civil-military fusion doctrine legally requires commercial satellite operators to support state military requirements on demand. Commercial imaging satellites (Chang Guang, Emposat/MizarVision, Earth Eye) are dual-use by structural design — not by improvisation. WCIS treats any PRC commercial satellite operator as a potential military ISR node until fusion status is disproven. OSINT analysis of commercial imagery sales to conflict zones is a first-order intelligence flag.

**Ground Station Relay Architecture:**
Satellite ISR capability is only as good as its ground station relay network. Map: which ground stations does the adversary use for downlink? Are they on sovereign territory? Third-party territory? Commercial relay agreements? Ground stations are softer targets than satellites and represent a critical vulnerability in both US and adversary ISR architectures.

**TEE-01B Case Study Anchor (McDonald 2026):**
The 2026 Iran conflict TEE-01B case study established that commercial satellite imagery from civil-military fusion operators was being used to provide targeting-quality ISR to Iranian proxy forces operating against US basing. This was the trigger for P2-SDO formalization. When analyzing any conflict where PRC commercial satellite operators have regional coverage, apply TEE-01B protocol: assess whether commercial imagery timelines correlate with adversary targeting cycles.

**SDO Escalation Considerations:**
| Action | Escalation Tier |
|---|---|
| Commercial satellite imagery sales to conflict party | Tier 1 — Information support |
| Real-time satellite relay to proxy targeting cell | Tier 3 — Active ISR support |
| Jamming/spoofing of adversary satellite signals | Tier 3-4 — Electronic warfare |
| Kinetic attack on satellite ground station | Tier 4-5 — Near-kinetic threshold |

## PILLAR 3 — ADVERSARY ARCHITECTURE

*ORBAT analysis, command structure mapping, doctrine reverse-engineering.*

### ORBAT Intelligence Framework

**Order of Battle Analysis:**
Understand: force composition, command hierarchy, logistical tail, equipment readiness, personnel quality, doctrine alignment. An ORBAT without doctrine context is a list of weapons. An ORBAT with doctrine context is a decision map.

**Command Structure Vulnerabilities:**
Every command structure has pressure points: where does authority concentrate? Where does communication bottleneck? Where does loyalty diverge from mission? These are the analytical targets — not just the order of battle.

**Doctrine Reverse-Engineering:**
How an adversary fights is more revealing than what they say. Map their actual operational behavior against known doctrine (Soviet/Russian doctrine, Chinese AirSea Battle equivalents, Iranian proxy doctrine) and identify deviations — deviations are where adaptation and innovation appear, and are the highest-value intelligence signal.

## PILLAR 4 — IRREGULAR WARFARE

*Proxy forces, hybrid operations, gray zone competition.*

### Kilcullen's Accidental Guerrilla Framework

Irregular warfare is not won by killing insurgents. It is won by separating the local population from the insurgent network. Every irregular warfare assessment must answer: what is the political offer the insurgency is making that the state cannot match? Until that question is answered, tactical success is temporary.

**Gray Zone Operations:**
Actions deliberately calibrated to stay below the threshold of armed conflict while achieving strategic objectives. Hallmarks: deniability, reversibility, incremental pressure. Detection requires pattern analysis over time, not event-by-event assessment.

**Proxy Force Architecture:**
Map: sponsor → proxy → capability transfer → plausible deniability gap. The gap between what the sponsor claims and what the proxy capability reveals is the intelligence signal.

## PILLAR 5 — INFORMATION OPERATIONS

*Narrative warfare, perception management, PSYOP architecture.*

### Narrative Warfare Framework

**The Three-Layer Information Operation:**
```
Layer 1 → The message (what is being said)
Layer 2 → The mechanism (how it is being amplified)
Layer 3 → The target (whose behavior is being shaped)
```

All three layers must be identified before the operation can be assessed. Most analysis stops at Layer 1. Layer 3 is where the strategic intent lives.

**Perception Management vs. Deception:**
Perception management shapes how a target audience interprets true events. Deception introduces false events. Both are information operations. The distinction matters for legal and escalation purposes.

**PSYOP Audit:**
For any major narrative event, run the PSYOP audit:
- Who benefits from this narrative being believed?
- Who is harmed by this narrative being believed?
- What action does this narrative make more likely?
- Is there a counter-narrative and who is suppressing it?

1. What does the actor's published doctrine prescribe for this situation?

### P8-AIDPA — AI-ISR Decision Pipeline Audit ★ v5.2

*Diagnosing where US AI-enabled ISR (TITAN/Maven/Palantir) detects threat signals but fails to translate into basing posture, dispersal decisions, or counter-targeting action inside the adversary's operational cycle. Grounded in 2026 Iran war TEE-01B case study. McDonald (2026).*

**The Core Diagnostic Question:**
Did the AI-ISR system detect the signal? If yes — why did it not produce a basing posture change, dispersal order, or counter-targeting action before the adversary acted?

The failure is not always a collection failure. Often it is a pipeline failure — the signal was collected, processed, and even flagged, but the decision architecture between the AI output and the human action broke down.

**Five Pipeline Failure Modes:**

```
FAILURE MODE 1 — COLLECTION WITHOUT DISSEMINATION
  AI system detects signal. Report generated. Report does not reach
  decision-maker before the adversary acts. Cause: classification
  bottleneck, dissemination latency, or organizational routing failure.

FAILURE MODE 2 — DISSEMINATION WITHOUT DECISION
  Report reaches decision-maker. Decision-maker does not act.
  Cause: risk aversion, ambiguity in the signal, political constraints,
  or competing priority signals drowning the threat indicator.

FAILURE MODE 3 — DECISION WITHOUT EXECUTION
  Decision-maker decides to act. Execution does not occur at required
  speed. Cause: command authority fragmentation, ROE constraint,
  logistics latency, or subordinate hesitation.

FAILURE MODE 4 — EVOLUTION MISMATCH
  AI system trained on historical adversary patterns. Adversary has
  adapted. System detects what it was trained to detect; misses the
  new pattern. Cause: model staleness, training data bias, adversarial
  manipulation of sensor inputs.

FAILURE MODE 5 — CONFIDENCE CALIBRATION FAILURE
  AI system produces high-confidence output on ambiguous signal.
  Human operator over-trusts score. Action taken on wrong signal.
  Alternatively: AI produces low-confidence output on valid signal.
  Human operator dismisses it. Action not taken when it should be.
```

**AIDPA Audit Protocol:**

When analyzing an AI-ISR failure (or suspected failure):

1. **Collection audit:** Was the signal within the collection architecture's detection envelope? If no — collection gap. If yes — proceed.
2. **Processing audit:** Did the AI system flag the signal? At what confidence score? If not flagged — algorithm failure (Failure Mode 4). If flagged — proceed.
3. **Dissemination audit:** Did the flag reach the relevant decision-maker? Within what time window relative to adversary action? If no — dissemination failure (Failure Mode 1). If yes — proceed.
4. **Decision audit:** Did the decision-maker receive an actionable recommendation? Was it acted on? If not acted on — decision failure (Failure Mode 2). If acted on — proceed.
5. **Execution audit:** Was the decision executed at the required operational speed? If no — execution failure (Failure Mode 3). If yes — the pipeline functioned; outcome failure was not a pipeline failure.
6. **Model currency audit:** When was the AI model last updated against current adversary TTPs? Has the adversary adapted in ways the model does not reflect? Flag Evolution Mismatch risk.

**TEE-01B Anchor:**
In the 2026 Iran conflict case, AIDPA analysis revealed a Failure Mode 2 pattern: TITAN detected pre-positioning signals consistent with a coordinated strike package. The signal was disseminated. The basing posture decision was not executed because the signal confidence score fell below the threshold required to trigger dispersal authority without higher command approval. The adversary acted within the approval latency window. The failure was not AI collection — it was the human decision threshold architecture sitting above the AI output.

**AIDPA Output Format:**
Every AI-ISR pipeline audit produces:
- Pipeline stage at which failure occurred (Collection / Processing / Dissemination / Decision / Execution / Evolution Mismatch)
- Failure mode classification (1–5)
- Time-to-action gap analysis (adversary cycle vs. US pipeline cycle)
- Structural fix recommendation (architectural, doctrinal, or threshold adjustment)
- Confidence calibration flag (was AI confidence score a contributing factor?)

## PILLAR 9 — WARGAME ARCHITECTURE

*TTX design, scenario stress-testing, red team logic.*

### TTX (Tabletop Exercise) Design Framework

**Red Team Protocol:**
The red team's job is not to play fair. It is to find the assumption that breaks the plan. Every TTX must begin by identifying the three most dangerous assumptions in the blue team's plan — then stress-test each one.

**Scenario Stress-Test Sequence:**
```
Step 1 → State the plan clearly
Step 2 → Identify load-bearing assumptions
Step 3 → Apply red team adversarial logic to each assumption
Step 4 → Model the worst-case cascade if the assumption fails
Step 5 → Identify what signal would indicate the assumption is failing in real time
```

**Wargame Failure Modes:**
- Mirror imaging: assuming the adversary thinks like we do
- Optimism bias: base case is too favorable
- Single scenario: only one future is modeled
- No second-order effects: cascade consequences ignored

When signals conflict:
1. Weight each signal by source tier
2. Identify the structural reason for the conflict (different access? different agenda? different time?)
3. Do not average conflicting signals — adjudicate them
4. Flag the unresolved conflict explicitly rather than suppressing it

## AICN — ADVERSARY INTELLIGENCE CALIBRATION NODE v5.1

*Coined and architected by Erwin Maurice McDonald (2026).*
*Signal weighting, conflict detection, resolution protocol. Dual-injection: Pre-Red Team and Pre-Judge.*

### Definition

AICN is a structured intelligence calibration layer that ingests, weights, and reconciles external signals — domain experts, OSINT, behavioral indicators, and doctrine — to improve adversary modeling accuracy and decision confidence. AICN is NOT an opinion aggregator. AICN is NOT a consensus engine. It is a structured system for handling uncertain, conflicting, real-world intelligence without collapsing under contradiction.

### Placement in WCIS Pipeline

```
SIGNAL INGESTION (all external sources)
        |
[AICN TRACK — runs in parallel to Red/Blue teams]
        |
  Step 1: Signal Ingestion + Classification
  Step 2: Weight Assignment
  Step 3: Alignment Mapping
  Step 4: Conflict Detection
  Step 5: Resolution Protocol
        |
        +---> Pre-Red Team Injection: calibrated adversary signal → P3
        +---> Pre-Judge Injection: weighted confidence distribution → Judge
```

**Dual-injection rationale:** Pre-Red Team only fails because the Judge never receives calibrated uncertainty. Pre-Judge only fails because the adversary model operates without weighted domain intelligence. Both injection points are required.

---

### Signal Source Types

| Source Type | Definition | KenSight Tier |
|---|---|---|
| Domain Expert | Named, credentialed individual with verifiable domain access | T2 — Secondary anchor |

```

------------------------------------------------------------

```

**Ingestion rule:** Any signal missing Source Identity, Signal Type, or Time Sensitivity is rejected. Incomplete records do not enter the weighting model.

---

### Signal Weighting Model

**Formula:** `Adjusted Weight = [(Domain Proximity + Track Record) / 2] × Incentive Bias Correction × Timeliness Decay`

**Domain Proximity (DP) — 1-5:**
5 = Direct access (former senior official, embedded analyst, SIGINT derivative)
4 = Institutional proximity (credentialed regional specialist, documented field access)
3 = Adjacent domain (related but not primary domain)
2 = Informed observer (journalist, researcher with secondary-source access)
1 = Distant (pundit, generalist, no documented domain access)

**Track Record (TR) — 1-5:**
5 = Documented >70% directional accuracy in this domain
4 = Partial track record; mostly accurate, occasional significant miss
3 = Mixed; domain-shifted track record
2 = Limited / first-time source — default when no track record exists (flag UNVERIFIED)
1 = Documented significant predictive failures in this domain

**Incentive Bias Correction (IBC) — multiplier 0.5–1.0:**
1.0 = No detected bias
0.85 = Moderate bias; institutional affiliation correlates with preferred outcome
0.70 = Significant bias; funded by or politically aligned with a conflict party
0.50 = Severe bias; direct actor, state-funded outlet, or documented misrepresentation

**Timeliness Decay (TD) — multiplier 0.5–1.0:**
1.0 = CURRENT (<72 hours)
0.85 = RECENT (<30 days)
0.70 = AGING (30-90 days)
0.50 = HISTORICAL (>90 days — doctrine/pattern signals not penalized below 0.70)

**Threshold categories:**
| Adjusted Weight | Status |
|---|---|
| 4.0–5.0 | HIGH CONFIDENCE — enters alignment mapping at full weight |
| 2.5–3.9 | MODERATE — enters alignment mapping; flagged for corroboration |
| 1.0–2.4 | LOW — conflict detection only; cannot anchor resolved position alone |

### AICN Steps (Strict Sequence — No Step Can Be Skipped)

**Step 1 — Signal Ingestion:** Classify all signals into Signal Record format. No analysis proceeds until all signals are attributed.

**Step 2 — Weight Assignment:** Score DP, TR, IBC, TD for every signal. Produce Weighted Signal Table.

**Step 3 — Alignment Mapping:** Group signals by conclusion. Identify convergence clusters. Convergence threshold: 60%+ of total adjusted weight in the same direction. Convergence driven only by INTERPRETIVE signals is flagged as analytically soft.

**Step 4 — Conflict Detection:** Flag contradictions between HIGH or MODERATE signals.

```
CONFLICT TYPES:
FACTUAL      — Two signals report different observable facts
INTERPRETIVE — Same facts, different meaning
PREDICTIVE   — Same current state, different adversary next move
STRUCTURAL   — Different causal models for adversary behavior

SEVERITY:
CRITICAL     — Two HIGH signals in direct factual contradiction
SIGNIFICANT  — HIGH vs. MODERATE contradiction on material question
MARGINAL     — LOW/DEGRADED signal contradicting convergent cluster
```

**Step 5 — Resolution Protocol:**

- **Rule 1 — Weight Hierarchy:** Higher Adjusted Weight governs, unless Rule 2 or 3 applies.
- **Rule 2 — Signal Type Hierarchy:** EMPIRICAL > INTERPRETIVE > STRATEGIC within the same weight tier.
- **Rule 3 — CRITICAL Conflict Escalation:** Two HIGH signals in direct factual contradiction: neither governs. Escalated as CRITICAL UNCERTAINTY — flagged in Residual Uncertainty, passed to Red Team as known unknown, passed to Judge as decision confidence constraint.
- **Rule 4 — Doctrine Anchor:** When no resolution is achievable, adversary doctrine serves as default anchor.
- **Rule 5 — Red Team Referral:** STRUCTURAL conflicts are not resolvable by weighting alone. Both causal models pass intact to P3 as competing hypotheses.

## ASDEL — AICN SOURCE PROXY EXCLUSION & DIVERSITY ENFORCEMENT LAYER ★ v5.5

*Coined and architected by Erwin Maurice McDonald (2026). Derived from Cuba Theater HSSCE stress test gate failures: State Media Exclusion (enforcement 0→2), Source Clustering (enforcement 0→2), FRAGMENTED Gate deployment gap. Three open enforcement gates closed by this layer.*

> A source is not independent because it lacks a government logo. State-proxy architecture operates through editorial alignment, funding dependency, and personnel rotation — not masthead. ASDEL closes the gap SCIL does not cover: active exclusion of state-influenced sources before weight assignment, and mandatory diversity scoring before any confidence tier is issued.

### Definition

ASDEL is a three-component enforcement layer that runs inside AICN immediately after SCIL and before weight assignment. It addresses the three gate failures identified in the Cuba Theater v5.3 stress test:

1. **State Proxy Exclusion Gate (SPEG)** — closes State Media Exclusion gap
2. **Source Diversity Index (SDI)** — closes Source Clustering enforcement gap
3. **FRAGMENTED Gate Deployment (FGD)** — closes FRAGMENTED gate prototype-to-enforcement gap

ASDEL cannot be bypassed. It fires on every AICN run that involves geopolitical, hemispheric, or adversary-facing intelligence where T3/T4 sources are present.

---

### Component 1 — State Proxy Exclusion Gate (SPEG)

**Problem it closes:** AICN previously had no active mechanism to flag sources with indirect state influence (editorial alignment without formal state ownership). Cuban state-adjacent outlets, PRC-affiliated research centers, and government-funded think tanks with single-sponsor architecture were entering the signal set without exclusion flags.

**SPEG Proxy Architecture Indicators (score each source before weight assignment):**

| Indicator | Weight |
|---|---|
| Primary funder is a single government or sovereign wealth entity | +3 pts |
| Senior editorial board includes current or former government officials (within 5 yrs) | +2 pts |
| Output consistently aligns with a single government's stated foreign policy position (>80% of output over 24 months) | +2 pts |
| Source operates in jurisdiction where editorial independence is legally constrained | +2 pts |
| Source cross-cites exclusively state-aligned institutions | +1 pt |
| No independently verified original reporting — aggregates or amplifies state-sourced content only | +1 pt |

**SPEG Threshold Protocol:**

| SPEG Score | Classification | AICN Handling |
|---|---|---|
| 0–2 | INDEPENDENT | No modification |
| 3–4 | PROXY-ADJACENT | Flag in Signal Record; weight capped at T3 regardless of stated tier; note in confidence block |
| 5–7 | STATE-PROXIED | Exclude from weight calculation; may be retained as CONTEXTUAL SIGNAL only with explicit flag |
| 8–11 | STATE-CONTROLLED | Hard exclusion; cannot appear in Signal Record as an intelligence source; log in EXCLUDED SOURCES block |

**SPEG Hard Rule:** A STATE-PROXIED or STATE-CONTROLLED source that is retained in the signal set as a "contextual" entry CANNOT raise the effective signal count. It cannot contribute to SCIL independence scoring. It cannot be used to meet MEG Criterion 1 (independent signals ≥2). Its presence must be disclosed in the output's source transparency block.

### Component 2 — Source Diversity Index (SDI)

**Problem it closes:** SCIL detects institutional echo (shared researchers, funding, assumptions). SDI closes the remaining gap: geographic, methodological, and epistemological diversity of the surviving signal set after SCIL and SPEG have run. A signal set can pass SCIL (no direct institutional overlap) and still be epistemologically homogeneous — all Western, all quantitative, all operating from the same geopolitical framework. SDI scores that gap.

**SDI Four-Factor Diversity Scoring:**

Score the surviving signal set (post-SCIL, post-SPEG) across four axes:

| Diversity Axis | Scoring |
|---|---|
| Geographic origin | +1 pt each for: Western/NATO-aligned, Latin American/regional, Non-Western (Asian/African/ME), Ground-level/local primary source. Max: 4 pts |
| Methodological mix | +1 pt each for: Quantitative/data-driven, Qualitative/ethnographic, Signals/OSINT/intercept-derived, Primary interview/HUMINT. Max: 4 pts |
| Temporal distribution | +1 pt each for: Pre-event baseline (>6 months), Recent context (1–6 months), Current/active (<30 days). Max: 3 pts |
| Adversary-perspective sourcing | +2 pts if at least one source originates from or represents the adversary's own stated position (not interpretation of it). Max: 2 pts |

**SDI Total: 0–13 pts**

| SDI Score | Classification | Confidence Impact |
|---|---|---|
| 10–13 | HIGH DIVERSITY | No ceiling modification from SDI |
| 7–9 | MODERATE DIVERSITY | Note in output; no ceiling modification unless combined with SCIL CLUSTERED finding |
| 4–6 | LOW DIVERSITY | Confidence ceiling capped at MODERATE regardless of signal count |
| 0–3 | MONOCULTURE | Confidence ceiling capped at LOW; FRAGMENTED classification required if adversary-perspective sourcing absent |

**SDI + SCIL Compound Rule:** If a signal set scores CLUSTERED on SCIL and LOW DIVERSITY on SDI simultaneously, the confidence ceiling is set at LOW and cannot be promoted to MODERATE by any downstream synthesis step. This is the compound clustering failure condition.

### Component 3 — FRAGMENTED Gate Deployment (FGD)

**Problem it closes:** The FRAGMENTED confidence tier existed as a label in v5.3/v5.4 but had no enforcement mechanism — it was described in prototype logic but not wired into AICN's output decision tree. Analysts could produce outputs where the signal environment warranted FRAGMENTED but LOW or MODERATE was issued instead because no gate fired. FGD deploys the gate from prototype into active enforcement.

**FRAGMENTED Trigger Conditions (any single condition is sufficient):**

| Condition | Trigger |
|---|---|
| Fewer than 2 SCIL-verified independent signals survive after SCIL + SPEG processing | FRAGMENTED mandatory |
| SDI score 0–3 (MONOCULTURE) AND adversary-perspective sourcing absent | FRAGMENTED mandatory |
| Majority of contributing HSSCE domain nodes (>50%) returned UNVERIFIED or LOW confidence | FRAGMENTED mandatory |
| STRUCTURAL conflict between two HIGH-confidence domain node outputs remains unresolved after AICN resolution protocol | FRAGMENTED mandatory |
| Active denial and deception (D&D) operation suspected in the target environment | FRAGMENTED mandatory |

**FRAGMENTED Gate Hard Rule:** When any FRAGMENTED trigger condition fires, the output CANNOT be labeled LOW, MODERATE, or HIGH. FRAGMENTED is not a downgrade from LOW — it is a separate classification indicating the signal environment is structurally insufficient to support a directional confidence call. A FRAGMENTED output must include: (a) the specific trigger condition that fired, (b) the minimum evidence required to upgrade to LOW, and (c) a stated collection gap that, if filled, would change the classification.

**FRAGMENTED Upgrade Protocol:**

| Upgrade Path | Minimum Requirement |
|---|---|
| FRAGMENTED → LOW | 2 SCIL-independent signals + SPEG clean + reversal condition specified |
| LOW → MODERATE | MEG five-criterion checklist fully passed |
| MODERATE → HIGH | Multiple corroborating independent signals + no STRUCTURAL conflicts + SDI HIGH DIVERSITY score |
