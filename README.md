## Strategic Intelligence Architecture

### Civilian-Safe · Open-Source · Confidence-Calibrated

HSSCE is a multi-domain strategic intelligence architecture designed to ingest, calibrate, synthesize, and operationalize geopolitical, economic, energy, logistics, and adversarial intelligence signals into executive-grade decision products.

This is not:

* a prediction engine,
* a partisan platform,
* a propaganda system,
* or an autonomous intelligence replacement.

This repository represents an attempt to build a structured, uncertainty-aware strategic calibration framework capable of:

* handling fragmented signal environments,
* reconciling contradictory information,
* modeling escalation pathways,
* and generating confidence-scored strategic assessments.

The system combines:

* **WCIS v5.2**
* **North Star Intelligence**
* **AICN Signal Calibration**
* **Multi-Agent Strategic Synthesis**
* **Executive Decision Superiority Architecture**

---

## Repository Purpose

The core research question driving this architecture:

> Under what realistic geopolitical, economic, and strategic conditions could Cuba transition from adversarial liability to hemispheric stabilization opportunity for the United States while reducing escalation risk and improving regional resilience?

The system is explicitly designed to:

* avoid ideological framing,
* avoid confidence laundering,
* avoid speculative certainty,
* and preserve evidentiary discipline.

---

## Architectural Philosophy

HSSCE operates on a foundational premise:

> Modern geopolitical failure increasingly occurs not from lack of data, but from fragmented signal environments, narrative contamination, confidence inflation, and institutional inability to reconcile contradictory intelligence fast enough to preserve decision superiority.

The architecture therefore prioritizes:

* calibration over prediction,
* signal weighting over consensus,
* adversarial modeling over narrative reinforcement,
* and uncertainty visibility over artificial certainty.

# Confidence Ceiling System

## Anti-Hallucination Enforcement

| Tier       | Definition                                                                |
| ---------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| HIGH       | Multiple corroborating signals. Minimal contradiction. Doctrine anchored. |
| MODERATE   | Corroboration present with unresolved conflicts.                          |
| LOW        | Sparse signal environment. Significant interpretive dependency.           |
| FRAGMENTED | Contradictory or insufficient signal density for reliable conclusions.    |
